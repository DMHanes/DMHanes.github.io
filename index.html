Experience



My work at JT4 focused on setting up analog test to confirm my team's adaptive filter simulations. I determined the loss on the machinery used and visualized simulation data using the Wigner-Ville Distribution, Frequency Time Analysis and the Power Spectrum visualization. Finally, I took data, validating an accurate test setup. I determined the linear relationship for the Signal to Noise ratio. Part of my responsibilities also included creating Measures of Performance and Test Matrices for the setup. I worked with spectrum analyzers, signal generators, FPGAs and software defined radios.


Sparx is a small engineering consulting firm that I've spent a long time at. Over my summer and winter breaks, I interned in the electrical engineering department. With engineers out on vacation, I was often tasked with picking up their jobs while they were away. I rewired the building's security system, spent 50+ hours soldering, validated and edited a myriad of schematics, as well as designed and assembled my own PCB from scratch. I had amazing mentors, and they left me stellar recommendations on LinkedIn (also visibile here).


I interned in the Honorable Jodey Arrington's Lubbock office when I was 16 years old, during my freshman year at Texas Tech. It is very different than anything I have done before. I was often on the phone with irritated contstituents and had to de-escalate the situation and calm their worries. In addition, I assembled correspondance for the congressman, as well as sent mail to constituents. I worked more hours out of all interns, and I learned how to balance challenging coursework with a time-consuming job. Now, I love challenges and working long hours (at least 10 hours a day).


Countertrace is the name of the chemical engineering development department at Sparx, equipped with a fully functioning wet lab. I worked with the luminometer and infrared spectrometer frequently, and maintained safe laboratory standards.

This was my final presentation at JT4, and a culmination of two months of hard work. The presentation includes both theoretical explanations and my analog testing results. I focused a lot on visualizing frequency-time plots. Enjoy!

8-Bit Breadboard Computer

Part of my Honors Modern Digital System Design capstone was assembling this Ben Eater inspired 8-bit breadboard computer. Out of all the groups, less than 30% were able to finish the project. My teammate had to drop the class, so I ended up being alone on this project for the majority of the semester. It had a working RAM, ROM, clock, registers and more.

Autonomous Rover

I programmed this rover with a Xilinx BASYS 3 FPGA board, and it was capable of autonomous motion, line detection, and ball shooting. I created several movement algorithms during the evolution of the project. Most of my code is available on my GitHub repository. In addition, I designed an overcurrent protection circuit and PWM control program.
